This is Nikola Tessla, but we call him Niko

Named for his seemingly boundless energy after the scientist, Nikola Tessla who was known for his work in energy.

He's mellowed out since he first arrived and is a bit more roly-poly than strictly bouncy, but he still meticulously maintains his rabbit furball collection.

Niko is a 4-5 year old ferret from Marshalls Ferrets (a large ferret breeder on the east coast) purchased from a really great local pet store. He is a fat, healthy, and happy guy who despite his weight, can still wiggle his way underneath the little shelf in our front walk. He loves to bound through tunnels and has competed in the Ferret Days Olympics (though he got nervous and wouldn’t actually come out of the tunnel which brought great amusement to the rest of the attendees).

He loves treats and will do tricks with great joy if you have some cat food or peanut butter for him to lick. His attention span is not nearly as great as his wits and charm, so training takes some patience on your part.

This is Peregrine Took, but he prefers Pippin

Like his namesake, Peregrin Took, Pipin has a joyful, playful take on life that sometimes looks like pure mischief ... unless, of course, you happen to see things from his perspective: 1 inch from the floor.

Pipin is a rambunctious little escape artist who laughs in the face of your attempts to contain him. You will find his cute little pink ears popping over the edge of your mixing bowl or out of your shoes when you least expect it.

His favorite flavor is rubber, velcro, and pens, especially if they’re secreted away in your purse. He particularly loves crinkly snack bags, and will help you sort and store your granola bars after sampling a few for quality assurance purposes.

Pipin is the playtime instigator. His best buddy and favorite playmate is Niko but he has been know to pounce on the cats and tease the dog. He will also come over and check in with you, and then pounce lovingly on your toes (no teeth, don’t worry) until you reach down and fluffle him or give him a belly rub.

Generally you will find him in nooks about 4 feet off the ground, or in the nearest fleece-lined container or purse. He loves the outdoors, digging under trees, and hiding in the clothes you leave on the floor. He loves his harness and gets excited when he hears the jingle bell.
